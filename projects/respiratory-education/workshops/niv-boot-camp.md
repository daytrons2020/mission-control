## Learning Objectives
By the end of this workshop, participants will be able to:

1. Identify appropriate candidates for NIV based on clinical presentation and ABG results
2. Select appropriate interfaces and fit them correctly for optimal comfort and seal
3. Initiate BiPAP therapy with appropriate initial settings
4. Troubleshoot common NIV complications including mask leaks and patient intolerance
5. Monitor patient response and determine criteria for NIV success or need for escalation

## Prerequisites
- Basic understanding of respiratory physiology
- Completion of "Oxygen Therapy and ABG Interpretation" module
- Current BLS certification

## Instructor Guide

### Key Teaching Points

#### Indications for NIV
1. Acute-on-chronic respiratory failure (COPD)
2. Cardiogenic pulmonary edema
3. Hypoxemic respiratory failure (immunocompromised)
4. Prevention of post-extubation failure

#### Contraindications (Absolute)
- Respiratory arrest
- Hemodynamic instability
- Inability to protect airway
- Uncooperative patient
- Facial trauma/surgery

#### Initial Settings
- IPAP: 10 cmH2O (titrate to 15-20)
- EPAP: 5 cmH2O (titrate to 8-10)
- Backup rate: 8-12 (if timed mode)
- FIO2: Start at 100%, titrate to SpO2 goal

#### Success Criteria
- RR < 24 within 1-2 hours
- Improved pH within 2-4 hours
- Decreased work of breathing
- Patient tolerance

### Common Pitfalls to Address
1. Starting with pressures too high (causes intolerance)
2. Poor mask fit (causes leaks and eye irritation)
3. Inadequate coaching (patient anxiety)
4. Delayed recognition of failure
