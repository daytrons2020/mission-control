## Workshop Flow

### Phase 1: Demonstration (20% of time)
- Instructor demonstration
- Key points highlighted
- Common errors discussed

### Phase 2: Guided Practice (30% of time)
- Participants practice with instructor guidance
- Peer observation and feedback
- Immediate correction of errors

### Phase 3: Independent Practice (30% of time)
- Participants practice independently
- Instructor circulates for feedback
- Self-assessment using checklist

### Phase 4: Competency Validation (20% of time)
- Return demonstration
- Competency checklist completion
- Remedial instruction if needed

## Workshop Types

### 1. New Hire Orientation Series
- **Duration:** 1 week
- **Topics:**
  - Department orientation
  - Equipment competency
  - Emergency protocols
  - Documentation standards

### 2. Annual Competency Workshops
- **Duration:** 4-8 hours
- **Topics:**
  - Ventilator management updates
  - New equipment training
  - Quality improvement initiatives
  - Regulatory compliance

### 3. Advanced Practice Development
- **Duration:** 2-3 days
- **Topics:**
  - Advanced airway management
  - ECMO basics
  - Transport ventilation
  - Leadership in respiratory care

### 4. Interdisciplinary Team Training
- **Duration:** 4 hours
- **Topics:**
  - Code blue response
  - Rapid response team
  - Communication protocols
  - TeamSTEPPS principles

## Best Practices

### Workshop Design (90-minute format - evidence-based)
1. **Brief didactic introduction** (15 min)
2. **Hands-on practice** (45 min)
3. **Peer feedback** (15 min)
4. **Wrap-up and resources** (15 min)

### Facilitation Tips
- Keep groups small (6-12 participants)
- Use mixed-experience groups for peer learning
- Provide immediate, specific feedback
- Allow adequate practice time
- Document competency achievement

### Evaluation Strategy
- Pre/post knowledge tests
- Skills checklists
- Participant satisfaction surveys
- 30/90-day follow-up assessments
- Impact on clinical outcomes
