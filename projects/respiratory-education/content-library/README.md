# Content Library Structure

## Respiratory Therapy Education Content Outline

This document provides a comprehensive content structure for respiratory therapy education organized by topic area and competency level.

## Core Content Areas

### 1. FOUNDATIONAL KNOWLEDGE

#### 1.1 Anatomy & Physiology
- **Respiratory System Anatomy**
  - Upper airway structures
  - Lower airway structures
  - Lung parenchyma
  - Pleural space
  - Chest wall mechanics
  
- **Respiratory Physiology**
  - Ventilation mechanics
  - Gas exchange (oxygenation)
  - Gas transport
  - Acid-base balance
  - Control of breathing
  - Cardiopulmonary interactions

#### 1.2 Physics & Chemistry
- **Gas Laws**
  - Boyle's Law
  - Charles's Law
  - Dalton's Law
  - Henry's Law
  
- **Humidity and Aerosol Therapy**
  - Humidity physics
  - Aerosol generation
  - Particle deposition

- **Imaging Studies**

## Content Level Classification

### Level 1: Basic (Entry-Level)
- New graduate RTs
- Orientation content
- Fundamental concepts and skills

### Level 2: Intermediate (Staff RT)
- General practice content
- Common clinical scenarios
- Standard procedures

### Level 3: Advanced (Senior RT/Specialist)
- Complex clinical scenarios
- Specialized procedures
- Leadership and education roles

### Level 4: Expert (Clinical Specialist/Educator)
- Cutting-edge practices
- Research and evidence evaluation
- Program development

## Content Formats

Each topic area includes:
1. **Didactic Content** - Presentations, readings
2. **Skills Videos** - Procedure demonstrations
3. **Case Studies** - Clinical scenarios
4. **Competency Checklists** - Skills validation
5. **Assessment Tools** - Knowledge tests
6. **Reference Materials** - Guidelines, articles
