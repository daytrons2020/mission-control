# New Student Orientation — 15 Minute Speaking Points
## [Hospital Name] Education Hospital

---

## Opening (1 minute)

**Welcome**
> "Welcome to [Hospital Name]. I'm [Your Name], and I'll be your orientation lead today. This will be quick — 15 minutes — but I want to give you the essentials that will set you up for success."

**One Question to Start:**
> "What's your biggest concern about starting clinicals here?" *(Listen to 1-2 responses)*

---

## The Big Three (5 minutes)

### 1. Patient Safety Is Everything
- Hand hygiene: before and after EVERY patient contact
- Two patient identifiers before any procedure
- Never ignore alarms — investigate immediately
- **"If you haven't been checked off on a skill, you don't do it alone"**

### 2. Professional Expectations
- Arrive 15 minutes early — no exceptions
- ID badge visible, clean scrubs, closed-toe shoes
- Phones on silent, never in patient rooms
- **"If you're on time, you're late. If you're early, you're on time."**

### 3. Communication Standards
- Introduce yourself to every patient: name, role, purpose
- SBAR for report: Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation
- Use teach-back: "Can you tell me in your own words...?"
- **Questions are encouraged — the only dumb question is the one you don't ask**

---

## What Makes Us Different (3 minutes)

**We're an Education Hospital:**
- Teaching happens at the bedside
- Expect to be asked questions — it's how we learn
- Mistakes are learning opportunities if you own them
- You'll start with observation, progress to hands-on as you prove competency

**Your Preceptor:**
- Your primary guide and safety net
- Regular check-ins — daily or per shift
- Ask them anything — that's what they're here for

---

## The Rules That Matter (4 minutes)

### HIPAA & Privacy
> "Never discuss patients in public — elevators, cafeteria, parking lot. No photos of patients, charts, or screens. When in doubt, don't post it."

### Documentation
> "If it wasn't charted, it didn't happen. Be objective, factual, and document in real-time when possible."

### Social Media
- Zero tolerance for patient information sharing
- Violations can end your rotation and career
- **"Would you want your medical story shared online?"**

### Substance Use
- Zero tolerance for impairment on duty
- Random drug screening
- If you need help, seek it before it finds you

---

## Your Support System (1 minute)

**Who to Call:**
- Clinical questions → Your Preceptor
- Emergency → Rapid Response / Code Blue
- After-hours concerns → Charge Nurse
- Education issues → [Coordinator Name]

**Resources:**
- Simulation lab for skill practice
- Library for research
- Student services for support

## Closing (1 minute)

**Final Encouragement:**
> "You're here because we believe you can do this. Clinical rotations will challenge you — that's the point. Growth happens outside your comfort zone. We're here to support you every step of the way."

**One Last Question:**
> "What one thing can I clarify before you start?"

## Speaker Tips

- **Stand up** — commands attention in a short meeting
- **Make eye contact** — builds connection
- **Use one story** — real example beats a rule
- **Pause for the question** — engages them
- **End on encouragement** — sets positive tone
