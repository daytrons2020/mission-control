**The Role of a Respiratory Therapist**

Respiratory Therapists are highly skilled allied health professionals who specialize in the assessment, treatment, and care of patients with breathing disorders and cardiopulmonary diseases. Their scope of practice encompasses:

• **Patient Assessment:** Evaluating lung function, oxygenation status, and ventilation through physical examination, arterial blood gas analysis, pulmonary function testing, and bedside monitoring.

• **Therapeutic Interventions:** Administering aerosol medications, chest physiotherapy, bronchial hygiene techniques, and oxygen therapy to optimize respiratory status.

• **Life Support Management:** Operating, managing, and weaning mechanical ventilators for critically ill patients in intensive care settings, including invasive and non-invasive ventilation modalities.

• **Emergency Response:** Participating in code blue resuscitations, airway management, and emergency intubation procedures.

• **Diagnostic Procedures:** Performing arterial blood gas sampling, bedside spirometry, and assisting with bronchoscopy and other pulmonary diagnostic tests.

• **Patient Education:** Teaching patients and families about chronic respiratory conditions, medication administration techniques, and home oxygen therapy.

• **Interdisciplinary Collaboration:** Working closely with physicians, nurses, and other healthcare team members to develop and implement comprehensive patient care plans.
