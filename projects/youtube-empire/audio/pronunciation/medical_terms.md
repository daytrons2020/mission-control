# Medical Terms Pronunciation Guide

| Term | Phonetic | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Heart | HART | Simple, clear |
| Artery | AR-ter-ee | Three syllables |
| Vein | VAYN | Rhymes with "pain" |
| Capillary | KAP-ih-lair-ee | Tiny blood vessels |
| Aorta | ay-OR-tuh | Main artery |
| Valve | VALV | Heart door |
| Chamber | CHAYM-ber | Heart room |

| Blood pressure | BLUD PRESH-er | Force of blood |
| Circulation | sur-kyoo-LAY-shun | Blood flow |

## Script Format

Use this format in scripts:
```
Your heart has special doors called valves (VALVS) that open and close 
to keep blood moving in the right direction.
```

Or for longer terms:
```
The trachea (TRAY-kee-uh), also called the windpipe, carries air to your lungs.
```
